<p><p>Perfusion imaging is a diagnostic technique used to assess blood flow in organs and tissues. There are three main types of perfusion imaging markets, including Computed Tomography (CT Scan), Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), and Nuclear Medicine. CT Scan uses X-rays and computer algorithms to create detailed images of blood flow. MRI utilizes magnetic fields and radio waves to generate high-resolution images. Nuclear Medicine involves injecting a radioactive tracer into the bloodstream and detecting it using specialized cameras. These methods help doctors diagnose various conditions by providing insights into blood circulation and tissue function.</p></p>
