<p><p>The market for wires used in energy transmission can be categorized based on the voltage levels they are designed to handle. Low voltage wires typically transmit electricity up to 1000 volts, while medium voltage wires are used for voltages ranging from 1000 to 35,000 volts. High voltage wires are designed to carry electricity in the range of 35,000 to 230,000 volts, and extra high voltage wires can handle voltages exceeding 230,000 volts. These different market segments represent the varying needs and requirements for energy transmission at different voltage levels.</p></p>

<p><p>The wires for energy transmission market includes two main segments: overhead and underground applications. Overhead transmission lines are suspended on tall towers and carry high-voltage electricity across long distances. They are cost-effective and easy to install, but prone to weather-related outages. On the other hand, underground transmission lines are installed beneath the ground, ensuring aesthetic appeal and protection against weather conditions. However, underground systems are more expensive to install and repair. Both segments play a crucial role in providing reliable and efficient energy transmission for various industries and consumers.</p></p>
