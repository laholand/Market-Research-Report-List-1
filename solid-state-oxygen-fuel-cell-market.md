<p><p>Solid State Oxygen Fuel Cell (SOFC) is a type of fuel cell that converts chemical energy from fuel, usually hydrogen, and oxygen into electrical energy through an electrochemical reaction. SOFCs can be classified into two main types: planar and tubular. </p><p>Planar SOFCs consist of flat layers of ceramic materials, including anode, cathode, and electrolyte, stacked together. They offer advantages such as higher power density and ease of manufacturing. </p><p>On the other hand, tubular SOFCs have a cylindrical structure, with the anode and cathode placed on the inner and outer surfaces, respectively, of a ceramic tube. They are suitable for high-temperature applications and offer better thermal and electrical efficiency.</p></p>
