<p><p>The citrus-based dietary fibers market consists of different types of fibers, namely pectin, hemicellulose, and cellulose. Pectin is a soluble fiber found in the cell walls of citrus fruits and is known for its thickening and stabilizing properties. Hemicellulose, another soluble fiber, is found in the cell walls of citrus fruits and provides various health benefits, including improved digestion. Lastly, cellulose, an insoluble fiber, is derived from the citric fruit peel and contributes to promoting bowel regularity and preventing constipation. These dietary fibers play a significant role in the food and beverage industry as functional ingredients.</p></p>
