<p><p>An aerobic septic system, also known as an aerobic treatment unit (ATU), is a wastewater treatment system that uses oxygen to break down organic matter, producing cleaner effluent. It consists of a primary tank, aeration chamber, and a final clarifier. <p><strong>The Aerobic Septic System Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Conventional Activated Sludge</li><li>Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor (MBBR)</li><li>Membrane Bioreactor (MBR)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The aerobic septic system market consists of three main types: Conventional Activated Sludge, Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor (MBBR), and Membrane Bioreactor (MBR). Conventional Activated Sludge systems use bacteria to break down organic matter in wastewater, requiring large tanks for the process. MBBR systems utilize plastic moving media that provide a surface for bacteria to grow and treat wastewater. MBR systems use membranes to separate solids from the wastewater, providing a more efficient and compact solution. Each type offers unique advantages and is suitable for different applications.</p></p>
