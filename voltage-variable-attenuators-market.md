<p><p>Voltage variable attenuators are electronic devices used to control the power level of a signal in a circuit by varying the voltage applied to them. Two common types of voltage variable attenuators are diode-based attenuators and MMIC-based attenuators. Diode-based attenuators use a diode to control the signal strength, while MMIC-based attenuators utilize monolithic microwave integrated circuit technology. Both types offer precise and adjustable attenuation levels, but diode-based attenuators are generally more cost-effective, while MMIC-based attenuators offer higher performance and wider frequency ranges.</p></p>
