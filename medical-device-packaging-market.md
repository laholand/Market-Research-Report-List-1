<p><p>The medical device packaging market offers various types of packaging options namely trays, pouches, clamshell, and others. Trays are commonly used for securing and organizing medical devices during transportation and storage. Pouches provide a sterile barrier for medical devices and are often used for sterilization purposes. Clamshell packaging consists of two hinged halves that enclose the medical device, providing protection and visibility. Other packaging options may include blister packs or boxes. All these types of packaging play a crucial role in ensuring the safety, sterility, and proper functioning of medical devices.</p></p>

<p><p>The medical device packaging market consists of sterile packaging and non-sterile packaging. Sterile packaging is designed to maintain the sterility of medical devices and protect them from contamination. It includes materials such as plastic films, trays, and pouches with features like seal integrity. Non-sterile packaging, on the other hand, is used for medical devices that do not require sterility. It includes materials like cartons, labels, and wraps, which provide protection, organization, and instructions for handling the devices. Both types of packaging play a crucial role in ensuring the safety and efficiency of medical devices.</p></p>
