<p><p>Infrared gas analyzers are devices used to detect and measure the concentration of certain gases in a given environment. They are available in two main types: open-path infrared gas analyzers and closed-path infrared gas analyzers. Open-path analyzers use a beam of infrared light that travels across an open space to detect gases, while closed-path analyzers use a sample cell or chamber to contain the gas being analyzed. Both types offer reliable and accurate measurements of gas concentration, but their usage depends on specific application requirements and environmental conditions.</p></p>
