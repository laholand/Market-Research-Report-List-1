<p><p>Active Modified Atmosphere Packaging (AMAP) technology is a packaging method used to extend the shelf life of perishable food products by controlling the gas composition inside the package. It involves replacing or modifying the air surrounding the food with a mixture of gases such as oxygen, carbon dioxide, and nitrogen, which helps to inhibit the growth of microorganisms and delay the degradation processes.</p><p>AMAP technology has gained significant popularity in the food packaging industry due to its ability to preserve the quality and freshness of the packaged products for a longer period. It is widely used for packaging fresh fruits and vegetables, meat and poultry, seafood, bakery products, and dairy products.</p><p>The market for AMAP technology is witnessing steady growth, driven by factors such as the increasing demand for convenience food products with an extended shelf life, the growing awareness among consumers regarding food quality and safety, and the need to reduce food waste.
