<p><p>Optical spectrometers are used in various industries to analyze and measure the characteristics of light. Atomic spectrometry focuses on studying the interaction between light and atoms, allowing for the identification and quantification of elements within a sample. This market covers techniques like atomic absorption spectrometry and atomic emission spectrometry. Molecular spectrometry, on the other hand, examines the interaction between light and molecules. This market involves techniques such as infrared spectroscopy and ultraviolet-visible spectroscopy, enabling the identification and analysis of compounds. Both markets play a crucial role in scientific research, quality control, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
