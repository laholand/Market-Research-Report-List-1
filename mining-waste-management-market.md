<p><p>Mining waste management refers to the proper handling and disposal of waste generated during mining operations. There are three main types of mining waste: waste rock, tailings, and mining water. Waste rock includes non-valuable rocks and minerals that are removed during excavation. Tailings are the residue left after ore has been processed, often containing toxic substances. Mining water refers to the water used in mining processes, which may become contaminated with pollutants. Effective management of these three types of waste is critical to minimize environmental impact and ensure sustainable mining practices.</p></p>
