<p><p>Hydronephrosis is a medical condition characterized by the swelling of one or both kidneys due to the buildup of urine. The treatment for hydronephrosis depends on the underlying cause and severity of the condition. In mild cases, patients may be prescribed medications to help alleviate symptoms and reduce the swelling. However, more severe cases may require surgical intervention.</p><p>Surgical treatments for hydronephrosis include:</p><p>1. Pyeloplasty: This procedure involves reconstructing or widening the narrow or blocked ureter, which is the tube that carries urine from the kidney to the bladder.</p><p>2. Stent placement: In cases where the blockage is due to a stone or tumor, a stent can be inserted to help relieve the obstruction and allow urine to drain properly.</p><p>3. Nephrectomy: In severe cases where the affected kidney is irreparably damaged and non-functional, it may need to be surgically removed.</p><p>The global hydronephrosis treatment market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
