<p><p>The biodegradable plastic packaging market consists of various types, including starch-based, polylactic acid (PLA), polybutylene succinate (PBS), polybutylene adipate terephthalate (PBAT), polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA), and others. Starch-based packaging is made from renewable resources with starch as the main component. PLA is derived from plant sugars and is compostable. PBS is produced from succinic acid and 1,4-butanediol and is both biodegradable and biocompatible. PBAT combines biodegradable and biobased polymers. PHA is naturally produced by microorganisms and offers high biodegradability. Other types include cellulose-based, PHB, and PCL plastics. All these options promote eco-friendly solutions in packaging industry.</p></p>
