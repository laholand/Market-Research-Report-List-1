<p><p>Variable hydraulic pumps are categorized into two main types: axial pumps and radial pumps. Axial pumps have a narrower design, offering higher pressure and efficiency levels. These pumps are designed to handle higher loads and pressures, making them ideal for applications requiring heavy-duty performance. On the other hand, radial pumps have a wider design, allowing for higher flow rates and lower pressure levels. They are suitable for applications where a constant flow rate is required, such as in power steering systems. Both types of pumps serve different purposes but are essential in various hydraulic systems.</p></p>
