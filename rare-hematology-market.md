<p><p>Rare Hematology refers to a branch of medicine that deals with the diagnosis, treatment, and management of rare blood disorders. The Rare Hematology market can be categorized into two types: Recombinant Factors and Plasma Derived Factors. Recombinant Factors are artificial proteins produced using biotechnology methods to replace missing or defective clotting factors in patients. Plasma Derived Factors, on the other hand, are derived from human blood plasma and contain naturally occurring clotting factors. Both of these market types aim to provide effective therapies for patients with rare blood disorders.</p></p>
