<p><p>High Fidelity Simulation (HFS) is a type of simulation that closely replicates real-world scenarios for training purposes. In the context of the market, there are two main types: software and anatomical models. Software refers to computer programs that provide virtual simulations, allowing users to interact and learn in a realistic environment. Anatomical models, on the other hand, are physical replicas of human body parts or systems, which healthcare professionals can use for hands-on training. Both software and anatomical models contribute to the advancement of HFS in various industries such as healthcare, aviation, and defense.</p></p>
