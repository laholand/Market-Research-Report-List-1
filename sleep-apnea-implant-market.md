<p><p>Sleep apnea implant, also known as a hypoglossal nerve stimulation device, is a surgically implanted device used to treat obstructive sleep apnea (OSA), a common sleep disorder characterized by interrupted or shallow breathing during sleep. The implant works by delivering mild electrical stimulation to the hypoglossal nerve, which controls the movement of the tongue and other muscles involved in breathing. This stimulation keeps the airway open and prevents the collapse that typically occurs in sleep apnea patients.</p><p>The sleep apnea implant market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. One of the key factors driving this growth is the rising prevalence of sleep apnea globally. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), an estimated 100 million people suffer from sleep apnea worldwide. Manufacturers are focusing on developing more efficient and user-friendly devices with improved features. These advancements are expected to attract a larger patient pool and drive market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the preference for minimally invasive surgeries and the increasing adoption of sleep apnea implants among patients who cannot tolerate continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) therapy are boosting the market's growth. CPAP is the most common treatment therapy for sleep apnea, but many patients find it uncomfortable and non-compliant.</p><p>In conclusion, the sleep apnea implant market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 13.1% during the forecast period. <p><p>Sleep Apnea Implant Market includes various types such as neuromodulator implants and tongue obstruction implants. Neuromodulator implants involve the insertion of devices that stimulate the nerves in the upper airway to prevent obstructions and maintain normal breathing during sleep. On the other hand, tongue obstruction implants focus on addressing the issue of tongue collapsing into the airway, by placing small implants into the tongue muscles to keep them tense and prevent blockage. Both implant types aim to provide effective treatment options for individuals suffering from sleep apnea.</p></p>
