<p><p>Postpartum bleeding, also known as postpartum hemorrhage (PPH), is a condition characterized by excessive bleeding after childbirth. Oxytocin is a hormone that helps the uterus contract and prevent excessive bleeding. It is administered through an intravenous drip or an intramuscular injection soon after childbirth. Other drugs that may be used to manage postpartum bleeding include misoprostol, ergometrine, and carboprost.</p><p>The global postpartum bleeding drug market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years.
