<p><p>The Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) Repair market is divided into two types: autograft and allograft. Autograft refers to a surgical technique in which the ligament used for repair is taken from the patient's own body, typically from the patellar tendon or hamstring tendon. This method is preferred due to its compatibility and reduced risk of rejection. Allograft, on the other hand, involves using a ligament graft from a deceased donor or a tissue bank. Both autograft and allograft have their advantages and considerations, and the choice ultimately depends on the patient's specific circumstances.</p></p>
