<p><p>Industrial radiography is a non-destructive testing (NDT) technique used to examine the internal composition of industrial materials. It involves passing high-energy radiation such as X-rays or gamma rays through a material and capturing the resulting image on a film or digital detector. This technique allows for the detection of defects, cracks, corrosion, or abnormal features that may compromise the structural integrity of the material. Industrial radiography is widely used in industries such as oil and gas, aerospace, manufacturing, and automotive.</p><p>The current outlook of the Industrial Radiography market is highly positive. In the digital market, radiographic images are captured using digital detectors, enabling real-time evaluation and easy storage and sharing of data. Film-based market, on the other hand, uses traditional X-ray film to capture images that require processing and developing. Both types have their advantages, with digital radiography offering faster results and enhanced image manipulation capabilities, while film-based radiography remains a common choice due to its familiarity and affordability.</p></p>
