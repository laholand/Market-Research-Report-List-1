<p><p>Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD) is a genetic disorder characterized by progressive muscle degeneration and weakness. It primarily affects males, with symptoms typically appearing in early childhood. DMD is caused by mutations in the dystrophin gene, leading to the absence or deficiency of dystrophin, a protein that is crucial for maintaining the structural integrity of muscle fibers.</p><p>The Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy Market, which includes various therapeutics and treatment options for DMD, is expected to experience substantial growth during the forecast period. The market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 8.9%, driven by factors such as increasing prevalence of DMD, advancements in drug development, and rising awareness about the disease.</p><p>One of the significant trends in the market is the development of gene-based therapies, such as gene editing and gene replacement therapies. These therapies aim to target the underlying cause of DMD by correcting or compensating for the genetic mutations responsible for the disease. Several clinical trials and research studies are underway to evaluate the safety and efficacy of these innovative approaches.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the adoption of exon-skipping therapies. These therapies involve the selective skipping of specific exons in the dystrophin gene, allowing for the production of a shorter but functional protein. Exon-skipping therapies, such as eteplirsen and golodirsen, have shown promising results in improving motor function in some DMD patients.</p><p>Furthermore, there is a growing focus on multidisciplinary care and management approaches for DMD.
