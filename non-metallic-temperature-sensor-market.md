<p><p>The non-metallic temperature sensor market includes two main types: thermistor temperature sensors and thermocouple temperature sensors. Thermistors are temperature-sensitive resistors that exhibit a change in resistance with temperature. They are commonly used in various applications, such as temperature control and measurement. On the other hand, thermocouples are temperature sensors that work based on the principle of the Seebeck effect. They consist of two dissimilar conductors joined together, generating a voltage proportional to the temperature difference between the two junctions. Thermocouples find extensive use in industries for accurate temperature monitoring and control.</p></p>
